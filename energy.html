Energy

As our nation’s energy needs evolve, affiliate family interests work alongside exploration and production, operating, midstream, and renewable providers in five states.

Oklahoma was the nation’s sixth-largest crude oil producing state in 2017 with five operable petroleum refineries with a combined daily processing capacity of almost 522,000 barrels per day, almost 3% of the total U.S. capacity. Oklahoma was also the fourth-largest natural gas-producing state in the nation in 2017, accounting for 8.6% of marketed production and ranked second in the nation, after Texas, in net electricity generation from wind.
